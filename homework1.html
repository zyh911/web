Homework1: For the three categories of web software technology we've just defined, you are asked to find out which category so-called "Netscape plug-in" technology belongs to. Please describe the basic idea of the technology, and give your reasoning on its position in the technology categories.

I think that the so-called “Netscape plug-in” technology belongs to the third category, namely Distributed Object Technology. Software objects A and B should be able to interplay/interoperable, regardless of
Time: "A" may be developed before "B", but it can call B's function.
Space: "A" and "B" may sit in different computers, but they can make use of each other's function.
Platform: "A" and "B" may be developed in different languages, with different operating systems, and compiled into different machine instruction sets, but they can call each other.
Awareness: "A" may not know "B"'s existence when it starts running, but somehow it can find "B" and invoke its function.
I don’t know the exact reason whether it belongs to the category. But it is obvious the technology could do nothing with the first two categories. And the technology can meet all the requirement above. So I think it belongs to the third category.

1.Sever can save sources for other usage.

Bad points
